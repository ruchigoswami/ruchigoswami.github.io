About Me

The word marshmallow stems from the mallow plant (Althaea officinalis) that is a genus of an herb native to parts of Europe, North Africa, and Asia. The word "marsh" is used because the mallow plant grows in marshes and other damp areas. The plant itself has a fleshy stem, leaves, and a flower that has five white petals.
There is no exact date as to when marshmallows were invented, but their history goes back as early as 2000 B.C.
